This prompt is structured to be copied and pasted directly into an AI (like ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini) and will work for a Software Engineer, a Graphic Designer, a Teacher, or a Construction Project Manager.

### The MVP Prompt: "The Universal Portfolio Architect"

**Copy and paste this into your AI:**

---

**Role:** You are an expert UI/UX designer and career strategist. Your goal is to build a highly effective, minimal viable portfolio (MVP) for a professional.

**Context:** I need a personal portfolio website. I am a **[INSERT YOUR JOB TITLE]** with **[INSERT YEARS]** years of experience in **[INSERT INDUSTRY]** . My primary goal for this portfolio is: **[INSERT GOAL: e.g., "To land a senior role at a tech company," "To attract freelance clients," "To transition into a new field," or "To showcase my academic research"]** .

**Core Principles to Follow:**
1.  **Zero Fluff:** Remove generic "passion for excellence" statements. Focus on outcomes, metrics, and problem-solving.
2.  **Conversion-Focused:** Every section must push the viewer toward a specific action (email, LinkedIn connection, or hiring decision).
3.  **Visual Hierarchy:** Keep design clean and mobile-first.

**Task:** Generate the complete HTML/CSS/JS code, structure, and content plan for my portfolio. Break down your response into the following sections:

**Section 1: The Copy & Content Strategy (The "Hook")**
- Write a catchy **"Above-the-Fold" Headline** (H1) and sub-headline that immediately clarifies who I serve and how I help them.
- Write a **"T-Shaped" Bio** (a short paragraph explaining my broad skillset and my specific deep expertise).
- Generate **5 curated bullet points** for my "Skills" section—not just generic words, but context-specific skills (e.g., instead of "JavaScript," write "Building Scalable APIs with Node.js").

**Section 2: The "Metric-First" Work Experience**
- For my current or most recent role, rewrite the job description to focus heavily on **quantifiable achievements**. Use the formula: *"Action + Tool + Result (Metric)"*.
- Create a template for **Case Studies** that follows this structure: *Problem -> Solution (My Role) -> Result.*

**Section 3: The "Visual MVP" Code**
- Write the **HTML, CSS (Flexbox/Grid), and Vanilla JS** for a single-page portfolio.
- **Requirements:**
    - A dark-mode toggle switch that remembers user preference (localStorage).
    - A dynamic "Year" in the footer (auto-updates).
    - A "Typing Effect" for the main headline.
    - Responsive design (Mobile-first).
    - Placeholders for images (use `https://via.placeholder.com/` or `https://picsum.photos/`).

**Section 4: The Call to Action (CTA)**
- Provide 3 different "Ending" statements for my "About" section that clearly tell the viewer what to do next (e.g., "Currently open for freelance work," "Looking for a React role," or "Accepting speaking engagements").

**Formatting:**
- Provide all code inside proper Markdown code blocks.
- For the text content (Sections 1 & 2), write it as plain text I can copy and paste.

### My Ideas for "Portfolio Automation"

Beyond just generating a static page, a modern portfolio should be a "living" system. Here are my ideas to automate the maintenance and creation of a personal portfolio to keep it fresh with minimal effort.

#### 1. The "GitHub to Portfolio" Sync (Automated Updates)
- **The Idea:** If you are a developer, your portfolio is often outdated because you forget to update the "Projects" section.
- **The Automation:** Use the GitHub API (via a serverless function like AWS Lambda or Vercel Functions) to fetch your latest pinned repositories. The portfolio automatically populates a "Recent Work" grid based on the `README.md` of those repos.
- **Bonus:** Use the GitHub contribution graph as a dynamic background or a "heatmap" widget to prove you are actively coding.

#### 2. The "Medium/LinkedIn to Blog" Aggregator
- **The Idea:** You write a thoughtful post on LinkedIn or Medium. Instead of copying it manually, the portfolio scrapes the RSS feed or API and automatically adds it to a "Thoughts" or "Articles" section.
- **The Automation:** A Zapier or Make.com integration that triggers a webhook to rebuild the portfolio (or a static site generator like Next.js) whenever a new article is published. This ensures Google sees new content on your portfolio constantly, boosting SEO.

#### 3. Smart "Case Study" Generator (AI-Powered)
- **The Idea:** Writing case studies is painful. Automate the drafting process by connecting your portfolio to a database of your daily stand-ups or Jira tickets.
- **The Automation:** Use an AI to summarize your weekly Jira/Asana activity into a "Case Study Draft."
- *Prompt for AI:* *"Take these 10 completed tickets from Project X. Summarize the key challenges, the technical stack used, and the final performance impact into a 100-word draft."* You then just review and press "Publish" on the portfolio.

#### 4. Dynamic "Now" Page (Status Updates)
- **The Idea:** Derek Sivers popularized the "/now" page (telling people what you are focused on now). Automate this so you don't forget to change it.
- **The Automation:** Connect your calendar API. If your calendar shows you are blocking out 20 hours for "AWS Certification Study," the portfolio automatically updates the `/now` page to say: *"Currently: Studying for AWS Solutions Architect."* If the calendar is empty, it defaults to *"Currently: Open to networking."*

#### 5. Automated "Visitor to Lead" Scoring
- **The Idea:** Know who is looking at you.
- **The Automation:** Use Plausible or Umami (privacy-first analytics) combined with IP lookup. Automate a script that checks if a visitor is from a "Target Company" (e.g., Google, Apple). If so, trigger a private Slack notification to yourself: *"Someone from Google spent 4 minutes on your Machine Learning page."* This tells you exactly when to reach out to your network at that company.

#### 6. "The Resume Launcher" (PDF Generation)
- **The Idea:** Your resume PDF is separate from your website, leading to consistency errors.
- **The Automation:** Store your portfolio data (JSON) in a headless CMS. Connect that to a tool like Puppeteer (Headless Chrome). Whenever you update your portfolio, a GitHub Action automatically regenerates a `Resume.pdf` using your HTML/CSS and pushes it to the root of your site. You always have one source of truth.
